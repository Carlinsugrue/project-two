Reflective Story

What a semester that has been… software engineering seemed to start off like a slap to the face, throwing most of what I knew about BIT papers out the window. When I was done with the last exam I told Adon “never again” his reply was “you’re not going to like project then” and like most things this semester, he was right, instead of a dainty slap I received a mental Batista bomb. Now I’m not saying that project one filled me with hatred (on the occasion) but it was one of the tougher papers I’ve had to slog through. But much like the quote from Albert Einstein “Adversity introduces a man to himself.” I feel I can take away a lot of valuable lessons now.

One of the hardest lessons I had to come to terms with after summarizing my semester was how much I could have actively done through the project to improve my environment and learning experience, to drown the reader in quotes I feel Teddy Roosevelt accurately portrays this when he said, “If you could kick the person in the pants responsible for most of your trouble, you wouldn't sit for a month.”

To start my semester in project one I had been assigned the task of implementing digital signage to service the display needs of the project room. This started out as research into free and open source digital signage solutions. This came back with Xibo which Lewis had already suggested, no surprises there, so it was off to start creating the initial VM, layouts and campaigns. Once I spun up a Xibo server at home and created the layouts I was quite proud! Threw in a few motivational quotes for good measure and it was ready for the project rooms viewing pleasure! Overall, I enjoyed my time messing with the Xibo CMS and had hoped to contribute to the open source project, that was, until, the following meeting that we had all been fearing (or at least just me) who would be tasked with the start-up company CodeLingo.

The meeting had begun and I had displayed my pride and joy Xibo to the congregation, with the assigned task complete and a body free to ponder another mystery, Adon glances at the board and finds a missing name next to CodeLingo. So, I was to be BIT Platforms Champion of Codelingo, no big deal how hard could it be? Hard. But it didn’t have to be. As I tried to implement the illusive CodeLingo server on the completely foreign op infrastructure there was always some small obstacle to overcome, sometimes I solved it post-haste sometimes I didn’t. I was working by myself, I felt secluded in my suffering, after all most problems that I encounter in life I could surly google to gain a few steps towards comprehension, but no this was completely virgin code, un googleable.

Most of my troubles related to the onprem solution on serval occasions where patched and fixed via a few updates released shortly after, I couldn’t have known that these problems weren’t originating from my side? It was a completely different environment?! But... that is no excuse, and I should have asked. The staff of CodeLingo where more than happy to answer my questions and besides, they write QUERY language for god sake! But even through all the frustration I was adamant to resolve problems I encountered.

Finally showcase day. Fortunately, when in dire need of assistance whilst still developing one of the final pieces of software (lingo builder) to display at showcase Adon in his wisdom tells me to swallow my pride and ask for the much-needed help, the helpful team at CodeLingo where right on hand to lend their knowledge and thus the showcase had somewhat of a demo.

In conclusion, there was a lot of anguish and despair strewn throughout, but these states of mental being where merely the result of ignorance. I aim to take these invaluable lessons with me into project two (should I pass) and strive to approach my prior work with more conviction and clarity.
